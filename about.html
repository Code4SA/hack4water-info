About Hack4Water

South Africa is in the midst of a severe drought, with sources calling it the worst since 1992. Five provinces, North West, KwaZulu-Natal, Mpumalanga, Limpopo and the Free State, have already been declared disaster areas, with food security threatened as affected farms are unable to harvest crops or feed their livestock. It is also forecast that food prices will suffer a significant increase should the drought continue.

To address this issue, the Department of Water and Sanitation (DWS), in partnership with the Open Government Partnership South Africa (OGPSA) invite you to participate and submit your water hack to some of the countries water and sanitation challenges.

A hack is an innovative solution to a tricky problem, like building a water leak detector from a smoke alarm.
